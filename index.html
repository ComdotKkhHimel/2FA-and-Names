Frequently Asked Questions

What is an IP address?

Your IP address is a unique numerical label assigned to your device when it connects to the internet. Think of it as your digital return address that enables you to send and receive data over the internet.

Whether you're checking emails, shopping, or chatting online, your IP address works tirelessly behind the scenes, allowing websites to know where to send the information you request.

Why does my IP address matter?

Your IP address plays several important roles:

  • It allows websites and online services to send information back to your device
  • It can reveal your approximate geographical location
  • It's used by some services to verify your identity or restrict content
  • It can be tracked by websites to remember your preferences

Should I change my IP address?

Changing your IP address can be beneficial for several reasons:

  • Enhanced Privacy: Hide your online activities from websites and your ISP
  • Access Restricted Content: Bypass geo-restrictions on websites and services
  • Avoid Tracking: Prevent websites from building a profile based on your IP
  • Security: Protect yourself when using public Wi-Fi networks

You can change your IP address using VPN services, proxy servers, or by reconnecting to your network with a new dynamic IP.

Why was my IP blocked?

There are several reasons why your IP address might have been blocked:

Shared Server with Compromised Websites

Your IP address might be sharing a web server with hacked websites. When multiple websites are hosted on the same server, and one is compromised, it can affect the entire server and all IP addresses associated with it.

Automated Security Measures

Many online services use automated security measures to protect against attacks. These systems identify and block suspicious or potentially harmful behavior, sometimes mistakenly blocking legitimate users.

Too Many Requests

Making too many requests in a short period may trigger anti-DDoS security measures. If your activity accidentally mimics a DoS attack, you might get temporarily blocked.

Note:

If you're concerned about your server being compromised, you should contact your web hosting company for further investigation.
